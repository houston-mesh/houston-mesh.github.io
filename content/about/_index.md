## Who We Are

HoustonMesh is a loose community of Houston-area volunteers, radio enthusiasts, preppers, hackers, and neighbors who believe communication infrastructure should be **open, resilient, and owned by no one**.

We're not a nonprofit. We're not a company. We're just people who think it's cool (and important) to build a city-wide off-grid radio network in our spare time.

---

## Our Technology

We build on two open-source platforms:

### Meshtastic
[Meshtastic](https://meshtastic.org) is open-source firmware that turns inexpensive LoRa radio modules into mesh networking nodes. Pair one with your phone via Bluetooth and you're on the mesh. No account needed, no login, no fees.

### MeshCore
[MeshCore](https://meshcore.co.uk) is a newer platform with a different approach to routing and infrastructure. We run MeshCore repeater infrastructure to complement our Meshtastic nodes and explore what each platform does best.

### LoRa Radio
Both platforms use **LoRa (Long Range)** radio on the **915 MHz ISM band** — freely available in the US, no ham license required. LoRa trades bandwidth for range, making it ideal for text messages that travel miles on a single battery charge.

## What We Value

- **No gatekeepers** — the mesh belongs to everyone who participates
- **Open source** — every tool we use publishes its source code
- **Disaster resilience** — the network works when the grid doesn't
- **No surveillance** — we don't log your messages or track your location
- **Low barrier** — hardware starts around $35, no license needed
